ABOUT US

Ayurherb is a company found by Vivek Chadha that offers consultation and herbal medication to anyone suffering from a Chronic disease or to someone looking to become healthier by making some tweaks in their daily lifestyle.

Vivek Chadha is well qualified ayurvedic medicine and holistic health professional with more than 29 years’ experience in helping people lead a healthy and joyful life. He is the founder-director of Ayurherb Canada and Chadha Integrated Clinic (India 1991).

He started his Ayurveda journey as a student in 1985. After graduating as an Ayurvedic Doctor(Bachelors of Ayurveda Medicine and Surgery), he started his clinic in 1991 running successfully to date. Later, he completed his DNYS(Diploma in Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences) from the prestigious Gurukul Kurukshetra. Before moving to Canada, Ayurveda Doctor Vivek Chadha was actively involved in different social welfare organizations. He conducted various medical awareness camps in associations with different governmental and non-governmental organizations. He delivered several lectures in different conferences of repute for betterment in healthcare.

His simple individualistic approach by modifying food and lifestyle and his unique thoughtful herbal combinations have benefited thousands of patients all over the world. His personal, emphatic, honest approach to help, everyone feels health and joyful; and his in-depth knowledge of Ayurveda will help you strive for healthy years ahead.

Ayurvedic Treatment

An Ayurvedic practitioner will create a treatment plan specifically designed for you. He’ll take into account your unique physical and emotional makeup, your primary life force, and the balance between all three of these elements.

goal of treatment is to cleanse your body of undigested food, which can stay in your body and lead to illness. The cleansing process—called “panchakarma”— is designed to reduce your symptoms and restore harmony and balance. To achieve this, an Ayurvedic practitioner might rely on blood purification, massage, medical oils, herbs, and enemas or laxatives.

In the United States, it’s considered a form of complementary and alternative medicine (CAM).
